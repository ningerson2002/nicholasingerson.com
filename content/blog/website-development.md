# Building a Personal Portfolio: My Journey with Hugo, PaperMod Theme, and Netlify

As a developer, having a personal portfolio website is crucial in showcasing your skills and projects to potential employers and clients. In this blog post, I'll be sharing my journey in developing my own portfolio website using Hugo, the PaperMod theme, and Netlify for deployment.

## Why Hugo and PaperMod Theme

I chose to use Hugo for my static site generation because of its ease of use and flexible nature. With Hugo, I could create markdown files for my content, making it simple to add new blog posts or projects. I also found the PaperMod theme to be visually appealing and highly customizable, which was important to me in creating a personal brand.

## Deploying with Netlify

After setting up my website locally with Hugo, I deployed my site using Netlify. The process was straightforward and seamless, and I appreciated the ability to continuously integrate and deploy my updates with just a few clicks.

## Home Page and Additional Pages

My home page includes my profile picture, along with links to my email, LinkedIn, and GitHub. I also have an icon linking to my resume, making it easy for visitors to get in touch or learn more about my background.

In addition to my home page, I have a Blogs page and a Projects page, each showcasing a stylized list of my content. When clicked, each item expands to reveal the full post or project details. To make it easy for visitors to find what they're looking for, I've included tags in both my blog posts and projects, which can be searched using the Search page.

## Maintaining My Portfolio

I'm continuously updating and maintaining my portfolio website as I build new projects and add new blog posts. The experience has been both challenging and rewarding, and I'm proud of what I've created so far.

## Conclusion

In conclusion, using Hugo, the PaperMod theme, and Netlify for my personal portfolio website has been a great experience. The combination of simplicity and customization has allowed me to showcase my work and skills in a professional and engaging way. If you're looking to create your own personal portfolio website, I highly recommend giving these tools a try.
